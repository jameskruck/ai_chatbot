Conceptual Framework: Customer Service Strategy

Why Customer Service Strategy Matters

Modern retailers face a critical challenge: how to scale personalized customer service in a digital-first world. The wrong approach can damage brand loyalty and increase costs.

📊 The Strategic Decision Framework

1. Analyze Current State
  • Volume & types of inquiries
  • Response times & satisfaction
  • Cost per interaction
2. Evaluate Options
  • Technology solutions (AI, chatbots)
  • Human resources (hiring, training)
  • Outsourcing partnerships
3. Consider Trade-offs
  • Cost vs. quality
  • Speed vs. personalization
  • Scalability vs. control

AI Chatbots in Customer Service

✅ What AI Chatbots Do Well

  • Handle repetitive, fact-based questions 24/7
  • Provide instant responses to common inquiries
  • Scale without proportional cost increases
  • Maintain consistent brand voice
  • Gather data on customer needs

⚠️ Current Limitations

  • Struggle with complex, emotional issues
  • May frustrate customers seeking human connection
  • Require setup time and ongoing maintenance
  • Need clear escalation paths to humans
  • Performance depends on training data quality

Case Study: FashionForward's Strategic Decision

📋 Your Assignment

Read the following business case carefully. In the next phase, you'll collaborate with an AI peer to analyze Jessica Martinez's situation and develop strategic recommendations.

The Decision

April 8, 2024, Toronto - Jessica Martinez, CEO of FashionForward T-Shirts, stares at 127 unanswered customer emails. Her board meeting is Thursday (3 days away), and directors expect her recommendation for solving the customer service crisis threatening their growth trajectory.

🚨 The Crisis

4.2 → 3.1 Customer satisfaction (6 months)
8 → 31 Response time (hours)
$8.7M 2023 Revenue at risk

⚖️ Three Strategic Options

🤖 AI Chatbot

Cost: $87K (2 years)

Timeline: 6-8 weeks

Pros: Scalable, 24/7, handles 65% of tickets

Cons: Limited empathy, setup complexity

👥 Team Expansion

Cost: $256K (2 years)

Timeline: 4-6 weeks

Pros: Personal touch, brand alignment

Cons: Higher costs, hiring challenges

🏢 Outsourcing

Cost: $383K (2 years)

Timeline: 3-4 weeks

Pros: Professional, immediate capacity

Cons: Expensive, less brand control

📊 Critical Data Points

1,850 monthly customer tickets
34% are sizing questions
$156K quarterly revenue at risk
28% customer retention (down from 34%)

💼 Board Expectations

"We can't afford to lose customers over service issues when acquisition costs are rising." — Jennifer Walsh, Board Member
"Customer satisfaction is a leading indicator. We need improvement in Q2 or we'll face funding problems." — Robert Kim, Board Chair
